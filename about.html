About Yorba

Yorba is a software group located in San Francisco's Mission District. Our primary focus is developing free applications for the GNOME desktop, and to this end we're incorporated as a non-profit company. Our work is supported by generous donations from users who appreciate our software. We also do consulting work to help make ends meet: we are experts in GTK+, GNOME, Vala and free software in general.

Come pay us a visit, learn more about working for us and read all about what we're up to.

Meet the Team

Adam Dingle

Adam founded Yorba in 2009. Before that, he worked as a software engineer at Google, where he helped develop indexing algorithms used in Google Desktop. He also lived in Prague for several years, where he taught computer science at Charles University. He holds computer science degrees from Princeton and Berkeley. He likes playing the piano and guitar and travelling to far-flung corners of the globe.

Jim Nelson

Jim joined Yorba in January of 2009. He's worked in the software industry since 1988 on a variety of operating systems and applications. He enjoys writing, baseball, and cocktails.

Lucas Beeler

Lucas joined Yorba in June of 2009, after finishing his computer science degree at the Univeristy of Michigan. At Michigan, Lucas did research on GPU-accelerated non-photorealistic graphics under Igor Guskov and graded papers for quantum computing pioneer Yaoyun Shi. He's an avid hiker and tennis player.

Eric Gregory

Eric joined Yorba in November 2010. He's been in the software industry since 2008, after completing his master's degree in computer science from San Francisco State University. In his spare time Eric sips espresso, develops a software library called Modipulate, and you may occasionally find him writing snarky articles for local blogs.

Clinton Rogers

Clint joined Yorba in January 2011, after working at various capacities in the software industry since 1999. His interests include ice hockey, music, photography, and developing games.

Charles Lindsay

Charles narrowly escaped the kraken of commercial web development to find peace on Yorba's calm waters in January 2012. He relishes the opportunity to advance the state of free and open source technology. He appreciates facial hair, digs guitars and the shredding thereon, and fears humankind's eventual demise under the slimy tentacles of an amassing legion of cephalopod infantry.

Laura Khalil

Laura has the distinction of being Yorba's first groupie. Since joining the team in January 2012, Laura manages Yorba's web design, QA and systems. In her free time she enjoys board games, giving unsolicited advice and wearing shoulder pads.
